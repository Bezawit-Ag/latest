About Habesha

Honoring our Bahirdar roots since day one

We are a family-owned kitchen balancing heritage recipes with crisp, modern presentation.

Our promise

We slow-simmer our wats, hand-grind our berbere and mitmita, and ferment our injera just as our grandmothers taught us. Every dish honors traditional Ethiopian techniques while we layer on seasonal produce, local honey wine (tej), and warm Ethiopian hospitality.

  • Direct partnerships with farmers across Amhara region
  • Daily-made injera from 100% teff flour, fermented for 3 days
  • Traditional coffee ceremony (buna) every evening
  • House-made niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) in every dish
  • Chefs trained in both traditional Ethiopian and modern techniques

Craft

We grind our own berbere and mitmita, make niter kibbeh daily, and ferment injera for three days using traditional methods passed down through generations.

Community

We host traditional coffee ceremonies, Ethiopian cooking classes, and support local farmers and spice merchants from Bahirdar and beyond.
